# Intro to Threats and Attacks

---

what is a Threat Actor ((TA) ?

threat actor is an individual, group or a organization that conducts malicious cyber activity.

- identifying and categorizing threat actors can be crucial role  for defensive planning and assessment
- different types of threat actors or groups have different types of motivation, targets and TTP

TTP   =  Tactics . Techniques . Procedures 

- this the what , why and  how  the threat actors actions

### **Breakdown:**

**Tactics = The "Why"**

- High-level goals during an attack
- *Example:* "Initial Access" (get into the network)

**Techniques = The "How"**

- Specific methods to achieve tactics
- *Example:* "Phishing" (to get initial access)

**Procedures = The "Step-by-Step"**

- Exact tools and steps used
- *Example:* "Send email with malicious Excel attachment pretending to be HR"

**APT = Advanced Persistent Threat**

**Sophisticated, long-term hackers** (usually nation-states)

---

### **Key Characteristics:**

**Advanced**

- Uses custom malware, zero-days, advanced techniques
- High technical skill

**Persistent**

- Campaigns last months/years
- Keeps coming back after being detected

**Threat**

- Organized, funded, motivated
- Usually state-sponsored

---

### **Famous APT Groups:**

**APT28** (Fancy Bear) - Russian

- Targets: Governments, elections
- Known for: Phishing, DNC hack

**APT1** (Comment Crew) - Chinese

- Targets: Intellectual property
- Known for: Industrial espionage

**APT29** (Cozy Bear) - Russian

- Targets: Governments, COVID research
- Known for: SolarWinds attack

**Lazarus Group** - North Korean

- Targets: Banks, cryptocurrency
- Known for: Sony hack, SWIFT attacks
